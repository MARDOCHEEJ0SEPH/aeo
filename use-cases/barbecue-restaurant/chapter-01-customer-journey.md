# Chapter 1: Understanding Your BBQ Customer's Journey

## How People Find BBQ Restaurants Using AI

Your customers' journey to your restaurant has fundamentally changed. Understanding how they use AI to make dining decisions is critical for your success.

## The Modern BBQ Restaurant Discovery Journey

### Traditional Journey (Pre-AI)
```
Hungry → Google "BBQ near me" → Click 3-5 websites → Read reviews → Decide → Visit
```

###Modern Journey (AI-Powered)
```
Hungry → Ask AI/Voice assistant → Get 1-3 recommendations → Visit top recommendation
```

**Key Difference:** From browsing to trusting AI's singular recommendation

## Your Customer Segments

### Segment 1: Locals (40-50% of customers)

**How They Search:**
- "Best BBQ near me"
- "Good brisket in [neighborhood]"
- "BBQ open now"
- "Where do locals eat BBQ in [city]?"

**AI Tools Used:**
- Google AI Overviews (60%)
- Voice assistants - Siri, Google Assistant (40%)
- Apple Maps (30%)
- ChatGPT for recommendations (20%)

**Decision Factors:**
- Distance (within 10 minutes)
- Current wait time
- Specific menu items
- Consistency (they'll return)

**AEO Priority:**
Dominate "near me" and neighborhood-specific searches

### Segment 2: Tourists & Visitors (30-40%)

**How They Search:**
- "Best BBQ in [city]"
- "Authentic Texas/KC/Carolina BBQ in [city]"
- "BBQ restaurants visitors love"
- "Where to get real BBQ in [city]?"

**AI Tools Used:**
- ChatGPT/Perplexity (50%)
- Google AI (45%)
- TripAdvisor AI features (30%)
- Hotel concierge apps (25%)

**Decision Factors:**
- Authenticity
- "Must-visit" reputation
- Tourist-friendly
- Worth the drive

**AEO Priority:**
Be cited as THE authentic BBQ destination

### Segment 3: Catering & Events (10-20%)

**How They Search:**
- "BBQ catering for 50 people"
- "Best BBQ catering in [city]"
- "BBQ restaurant with large groups"
- "BBQ delivery for office party"

**AI Tools Used:**
- ChatGPT for vendor recommendations
- Google Business search
- Specialized catering platforms

**Decision Factors:**
- Group capacity
- Catering menu options
- Reviews for events
- Pricing transparency

**AEO Priority:**
Appear in catering-specific searches

## Customer Journey Mapping

### Phase 1: Awareness (AI Discovery)

**Trigger:** Craving BBQ / In town visiting / Planning event

**Action:** Ask AI or voice assistant

**AI Response Determines:** Whether they hear about you

**Your Goal:** Be mentioned in AI response

**Content Needed:**
- Strong Google Business Profile
- Location pages on website
- Menu with descriptions
- Reviews mentioning specific dishes

### Phase 2: Consideration (Validation)

**Trigger:** AI mentioned your restaurant

**Action:**
- "Tell me more about [Your Restaurant]"
- Check Google Maps
- Look at photos
- Read recent reviews

**Your Goal:** Confirm AI's recommendation

**Content Needed:**
- High-quality food photos
- Updated menu with prices
- Clear hours and location
- Recent positive reviews

### Phase 3: Decision (Commitment)

**Trigger:** Convinced to visit

**Action:**
- Get directions
- Check wait time
- Maybe call ahead
- Head to restaurant

**Your Goal:** Make it easy

**Content Needed:**
- Accurate Google Maps listing
- Click-to-call phone number
- Current wait time (if possible)
- Parking information

### Phase 4: Experience (Visit)

**In-Person Experience**

**Amplification Opportunity:**
- "Tell friends about this place"
- "Ask me to review on Google"
- "Share on social media"

**Your Goal:** Create reviewers who mention specific details AI will cite

## Geographic Targeting Strategy

### Neighborhood-Level Optimization

**If you're in Downtown Austin:**

**Target These Phrases:**
- "BBQ in downtown Austin"
- "BBQ near 6th Street"
- "BBQ walking distance from Convention Center"
- "BBQ in Austin CBD"

**Content Strategy:**
Create location page mentioning:
- Exact neighborhood
- Nearby landmarks
- Walking distance from popular spots
- Parking options in the area

### City-Level Positioning

**For "Austin BBQ" searches:**

**Positioning Options:**
1. **The Authentic Choice:** "Traditional Texas BBQ since [year]"
2. **The Innovation Play:** "Modern take on classic BBQ"
3. **The Specialty Angle:** "Known for award-winning brisket"
4. **The Value Position:** "Best BBQ for families"

**Choose ONE clear position**

### Competitive Set Understanding

**Who are you compared to:**
- Identify top 3-5 BBQ competitors in your area
- What are they known for?
- What's your differentiator?

**Example:**
- Competitor A: Known for ribs
- Competitor B: Family-style, big portions
- Competitor C: Upscale, expensive
- **You:** Best brisket, reasonable prices, quick service

**Your AEO Content:** Emphasize your differentiator

## Mobile Search Behavior

### 80% of "Near Me" Searches Happen on Mobile

**Mobile User Mindset:**
- Wants answer NOW
- Won't read long content
- Clicks call or directions immediately
- Makes decision within 30 seconds

**Mobile Optimization Priorities:**
1. Fast-loading website (under 3 seconds)
2. Click-to-call phone number prominent
3. "Get Directions" button obvious
4. Hours displayed immediately
5. Menu accessible in 1 tap

## Seasonal & Time-Based Patterns

### Peak BBQ Search Times

**Days:**
- Friday-Sunday (weekend dining)
- Holidays (Memorial Day, July 4th, Labor Day)

**Times:**
- Lunch: 11am-1pm searches spike
- Dinner: 5pm-7pm searches spike
- Late night: 8pm+ for "BBQ open late"

**Strategy:**
- Update hours accurately
- Mark special holiday hours
- Note late-night availability if applicable

### Weather-Based Searches

**Good Weather:**
- "BBQ with patio"
- "Outdoor BBQ dining"

**Bad Weather:**
- "BBQ delivery"
- "BBQ takeout"

**Your Content:** Mention both options
