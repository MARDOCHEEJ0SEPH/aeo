# Chapter 2: LinkedIn Articles for AEO
## Building Professional Authority Through Long-Form Content

LinkedIn Articles are one of the most powerful AEO tools available. They combine the authority of a professional network with the discoverability of a publishing platform. When optimized correctly, LinkedIn Articles get indexed by Google, cited by AI engines, and distributed to your professional network simultaneously.

## Why LinkedIn Articles Excel at AEO

### 1. Dual Indexing Power
- **LinkedIn Search:** Internal algorithm surfaces your content to relevant professionals
- **Google Search:** Articles are fully indexed and rank in Google results
- **AI Engines:** ChatGPT, Perplexity, and others cite LinkedIn articles as authoritative sources

### 2. Built-In Authority Signals
- Your professional credentials are attached to every article
- Endorsements and recommendations transfer credibility
- Network engagement signals quality to algorithms
- LinkedIn's domain authority boosts SEO

### 3. Long-Shelf Life
Unlike regular LinkedIn posts (48-hour lifespan), articles remain discoverable indefinitely. An article written today can generate leads years later.

### Title Optimization

LinkedIn articles live or die by their titles. AI engines and humans both judge relevance instantly.

**Title Formula for AEO:**
`[How to/What/Why] + [Specific Outcome] + [Target Audience] + [Timeframe/Context]`

**Examples:**

‚ùå **Bad:** "Marketing Tips"
- Too vague
- No question answered
- No target audience

‚úÖ **Good:** "How B2B SaaS Companies Generate 50+ Qualified Leads Per Month (Without Paid Ads)"
- Specific outcome (50+ leads)
- Target audience (B2B SaaS)
- Method (without paid ads)
- Answers "how to" question

**More AEO-Optimized Titles:**

1. "Why 73% of Remote Teams Fail at Async Communication (And How to Fix It)"
2. "The Complete Guide to Pricing Consulting Services: Data from 500+ Consultants"
3. "How to Reduce Customer Churn by 40%: Lessons from 10 Years at [Company]"
4. "What First-Time Founders Get Wrong About Product-Market Fit"
5. "5 Financial Metrics Every SaaS CFO Should Track (But Most Don't)"

### Content Depth Requirements

AI engines favor comprehensive, well-researched content.

**Minimum Standards:**
- **Word Count:** 1,200-1,800 words (sweet spot for AEO)
- **Headers:** 4-6 clear section headers (H2 level in editor)
- **Data Points:** Include 3-5 statistics or research findings
- **Examples:** 2-3 real-world case studies or examples
- **Visuals:** 2-4 images, screenshots, or charts
- **Links:** 3-5 citations to authoritative sources
- **Lists:** Use bullet points and numbered lists liberally

LinkedIn's editor is basic, but you can still create scannable, AEO-friendly content.

**Formatting Tips:**

- **Numbered lists for steps:** AI engines love sequential information
- **Add relevant images:** Break up text, increase time on page

### SEO Elements (Yes, They Matter on LinkedIn)

**1. First 150 Characters Are Critical**
LinkedIn uses the first ~150 characters as the preview/meta description. Make them count.

‚ùå **Bad Opening:**
"In this article, I'm going to talk about some strategies that I've learned over the years about marketing..."

‚úÖ **Good Opening:**
"73% of B2B buyers research solutions on LinkedIn before contacting sales. Here's how to ensure they find your content first."

**2. Strategic Keyword Placement**
- Title: Primary keyword
- First paragraph: Primary keyword + 1-2 related keywords
- Headers: Variations of target keywords
- Throughout: Natural keyword usage (don't stuff)

**3. Internal and External Links**
- Link to your other LinkedIn articles (builds topical authority)
- Link to authoritative sources (signals quality)
- Link to your website/landing page (only 1-2 times, be subtle)

### Visual Elements That Boost AEO

**1. Featured Image**
LinkedIn lets you add one featured image. Make it count:
- **Custom graphic** with the article title
- **High-quality screenshot** of key data
- **Professional headshot** with quote overlay
- **Infographic** summarizing key points

**Specs:** 1200 x 627 pixels (same as OG image)

**2. In-Article Images**
Add images every 400-500 words:
- Screenshots of tools/processes
- Data visualizations
- Charts and graphs
- Relevant stock photos (sparingly)

**Pro Tip:** Use alt text! While LinkedIn doesn't have a formal alt text field, describe images in the caption.

**3. Embedded Media**
- SlideShare presentations
- YouTube videos (your own)
- Twitter embeds (relevant threads)

### Common Mistakes That Kill Your Results

**Mistake #1: Writing for Everyone**

Bad title: "10 Marketing Tips"
Good title: "How Series B SaaS Companies Reduce CAC by 40%"

Be specific. Repel the wrong audience. Attract the right one.

**Mistake #2: No Clear CTA**

Every article should end with ONE clear next step:
‚Ä¢ Download a resource
‚Ä¢ Book a consultation
‚Ä¢ Subscribe to newsletter
‚Ä¢ Connect with you

Don't be shy. You provided value. Ask for something in return.

**Mistake #3: Posting and Ghosting**

The first 90 minutes after publishing are critical.

Do this:
‚úì Share to your feed immediately
‚úì Respond to every comment within 1 hour
‚úì Share in 2-3 relevant LinkedIn groups
‚úì Send to 5-10 connections via DM: "Would love your thoughts on this"

Engagement begets engagement. Prime the pump.

**Mistake #4: Inconsistency**

Publishing 3 articles in one week, then nothing for a month kills your momentum.

Commit to a sustainable cadence:
‚Ä¢ 1x per week = Good
‚Ä¢ 2x per week = Great
‚Ä¢ 3x per week = Only if you can maintain it

Better to publish weekly for a year than daily for a month.
